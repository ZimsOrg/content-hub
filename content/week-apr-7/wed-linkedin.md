## Title: Anthropic built an AI tool called CASH. It runs their growth experiments autonomously. They went from $1B to $19B in 14 months.

Most companies run A/B tests manually. A growth team designs the experiment, sets parameters, waits for results, analyzes data, decides what to do next.

Anthropic built an internal AI tool called CASH that does all of that by itself.

CASH designs growth experiments. Sets the parameters. Analyzes the results. Recommends the next move. All autonomously.

They're using their own AI to grow their own AI. And the results speak for themselves: $1 billion to $19 billion ARR in 14 months.

That's not incremental optimization. That's a completely different model for how growth works.

Here's what makes their approach unusual:

→ They index 70/30 toward big bets. Most growth teams do the opposite — 70% safe incremental tests, 30% big swings. Anthropic flipped it. They'd rather make 3 bold bets that might fail than run 30 tiny A/B tests that move the needle 0.3%.

→ CASH removes the human bottleneck from experimentation. When AI designs and runs experiments 24/7, you don't need to wait for someone to analyze last week's results before starting the next test.

→ Their Head of Growth thinks the PM-to-engineer ratio might need to flip. More product thinkers, fewer code writers. Because when AI makes engineers exponentially more productive, the bottleneck moves to "what should we build?" not "can we build it?"

Why this matters for everyone, not just Anthropic:

If you're building a product: You don't need a 10-person growth team anymore. You need one AI agent with a clear hypothesis and access to your data.

If you're in growth: CASH is the blueprint. AI that designs and runs its own experiments is coming to every growth team. Your job shifts from "run the test" to "decide which bets are worth making."

If you're a solo builder: This is the best part. The same pattern works at any scale. One person with the right AI tooling can run more growth experiments than a team of 5 did two years ago.

The competitive advantage isn't better AI. It's better leverage.
