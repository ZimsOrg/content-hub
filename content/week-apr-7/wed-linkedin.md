# Wednesday LinkedIn — Apr 9

## Title: Anthropic grew from $1B to $19B in 14 months. The growth tactic nobody's talking about.

Anthropic just hit $19 billion in ARR. From $1 billion. In 14 months.

Everyone's focused on the model quality. "Claude is so good." "Opus is amazing." Sure. But that's not why they're growing this fast.

The real story is buried in a recent interview with their Head of Growth.

They built an internal tool called CASH.

It lets Claude — their own AI — autonomously run growth experiments. Design the test. Set the parameters. Analyze the results. Recommend the next move.

Read that again. They're using AI to grow the AI.

But here's the part that made me rethink my own approach:

They index 70/30 toward big bets.

Most growth teams do the opposite — 70% incremental optimization, 30% big swings. Anthropic flipped it. They'd rather make 3 big bets that might fail than run 30 tiny A/B tests that move the needle 0.3%.

Why this matters for everyone, not just Anthropic:

→ If you're building a product: Are you running 30 safe experiments or 3 bold ones? The math changes when you have AI running the experiments for you.

→ If you're in growth: The "CASH" model is coming for every team. AI that designs and analyzes its own experiments. Your job shifts from "run the test" to "decide which tests are worth running."

→ If you're a solo builder: This is the best part. You don't need a growth team. You need one AI agent with a clear hypothesis and access to your data.

The other bombshell from the interview: Amol thinks the ratio of PMs to engineers might need to flip. More product thinkers, fewer code writers. Because AI makes engineers exponentially more productive, so the bottleneck moves to "what should we build?" not "can we build it?"

That's the real shift happening. Not better AI. Better leverage.
