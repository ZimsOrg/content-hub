## Title: The pattern that every serious AI workflow converges on

LinkedIn's editor uses it.
Stripe's engineering team uses it.
I use it every night building side projects.

Two agents. One builds. One reviews. You manage.

It sounds simple. It is simple. But the difference in output quality is massive.

Here's why it works:

→ A single AI agent will confidently write beautiful code that breaks in production. It can't genuinely critique its own work.

→ Two agents with different roles catch each other's blind spots. The builder optimizes for "get it done." The reviewer optimizes for "get it right."

→ The tension between those two goals is where the quality comes from.
