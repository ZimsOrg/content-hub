# Monday LinkedIn — Apr 7

## Title: I run a 6-agent AI team that builds my side projects. Here's what that actually looks like.

Everyone's talking about AI agents. Most of it is vaporware demos and Twitter hype.

So let me show you what it actually looks like when you use them every day.

I set up a swarm of 6 specialized AI agents using Claude Code and Codex. Each one has a specific job:

→ Bolt — the builder. Writes code, scaffolds apps, ships features.
→ Hawk — quality control. Reviews every PR, catches bugs before they ship.
→ Scout — researcher. Scans markets, competitors, trends. Brings me data, not opinions.
→ Pixel — UI/design. Handles layouts, components, responsive fixes.
→ Quill — content. Writes posts, docs, marketing copy.
→ Shield — security. Audits code, checks dependencies, flags risks.

I'm the manager. I assign work, review output, break ties, and make the calls.

Here's what a typical evening looks like:

7pm: I open my laptop and tell Bolt to scaffold a new feature based on a spec I wrote over lunch.

7:15pm: While Bolt works, I have Scout research competitor pricing for a different project.

7:30pm: Bolt finishes. I send the code to Hawk for review.

7:45pm: Hawk flags two issues. I send the feedback back to Bolt with instructions to fix.

8pm: Clean code. I merge and deploy.

Total hands-on time: maybe 45 minutes. Shipped a real feature.

The honest part nobody tells you:

It's not magic. You still need to think clearly about what you want built. The agents don't replace your judgment — they replace your typing. You're the architect, they're the builders.

And they mess up. Regularly. Hawk exists because Bolt will confidently write code that looks great but breaks edge cases. The "dual agent" pattern (builder + reviewer) is not optional — it's the whole game.

But when the system works? One person shipping at the speed of a small team.

That's not a flex. That's just the reality of these tools in 2026.

What's your AI setup look like? I'm genuinely curious who else is doing this.
