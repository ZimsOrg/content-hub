# LinkedIn Post — DoorDash Interviews

## Title: DoorDash just killed the coding interview. Every engineer should pay attention.

DoorDash just announced they are rebuilding their entire engineering interview process around AI.

No more algorithm drills. No more memorizing binary tree traversals. No more pretending engineers don't use AI at work.

Instead: a 60-minute AI-assisted working session. On your own machine. With your own tools.

Here is what their new interview actually looks like:

→ You bring your own laptop and IDE
→ You use whatever AI tools you want — Cursor, Claude Code, Codex, all features enabled
→ They give you a realistic task (build an order dispatch system, automate a support flow)
→ You share your screen and narrate your thinking as you work
→ They watch HOW you use AI, not WHETHER you can code without it

The skills they are testing now:

1. Can you get unblocked in an unfamiliar codebase without hand-holding?
2. Can you turn ambiguity into a clear technical plan?
3. Can you verify AI output instead of blindly trusting it?
4. Can you make pragmatic tradeoffs under real constraints?
5. Can you communicate your reasoning clearly while you work?

Read that list again. Not one of those skills is about writing code from scratch.

This is exactly what I have been experiencing building side projects with AI agents. The valuable skill is not typing code. It is directing AI, verifying output, and making good decisions about what to build.

Their quote that stuck with me: "We aren't interested in winning an arms race against AI cheating. We are optimizing for people who want to build, ship, and own outcomes."

What this means for you:

If you are spending weekends grinding LeetCode, stop. The companies that matter are moving past that.

If you are learning how to use AI coding tools deeply — how to prompt well, how to verify output, how to debug AI mistakes — you are building the skills that will actually matter in interviews going forward.

DoorDash is not the first. They will not be the last.

The question is: are you preparing for the interview process of 2024, or 2026?

What do you think — should more companies follow DoorDash's lead?
