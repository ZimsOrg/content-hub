# Friday LinkedIn — Apr 11

## Title: 95% of his code. From his phone.

Simon Willison — creator of Django — recently shared that he writes 95% of his code from his phone.

Not notes. Not pseudocode. Shipping code.

He describes what he wants. The AI agent builds it. He reviews and ships.

But here's what made me uncomfortable:

He thinks mid-career engineers are MORE at risk from AI than juniors.

→ Juniors are learning with AI from day one. It's native to them.
→ Mid-career engineers have deeply ingrained habits. Open file, type code, debug manually. These were advantages. Now they're friction.
→ Senior architects who think in systems are fine.

The dangerous middle: experienced enough to be set in your ways, not senior enough that your value is purely strategic.

I felt this in myself. My first instinct is still to open VS Code. But the fastest path now is to describe what I want and let the agent build it.

The skill shift:
Old: "Can you write clean code?"
New: "Can you direct AI and catch what it misses?"

Three things I'm doing about it:
1. Starting every project by describing it in plain language first
2. Using a builder-reviewer pattern (two agents checking each other)
3. Investing in architecture thinking — the last thing AI replaces

Are you adapting, or still coding like two years ago?
